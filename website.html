Understand it
Four pathways — they combine, not compete

Lifestyle change is the foundation under all of them; medications, endoscopic procedures and surgery are added tools chosen with your doctor based on your health and goals.

🥗Pathway 1

Dietary & lifestyle

The foundation for everyone, and enough on its own for many. The goal is not a short, punishing diet but a comfortable, sustainable way of eating and moving. Even a gradual 5–10% loss meaningfully improves blood sugar, blood pressure, cholesterol, joint pain, sleep apnoea and fatty liver.

  • More protein and vegetables; fewer sugary drinks and refined snacks.
  • Aim for 150+ minutes of activity a week, and protect 7–9 hours of sleep.
💊Pathway 2

Medical — medications

Prescription treatments for when lifestyle change alone isn't enough — usually considered at a higher BMI or with weight-related conditions. Modern options are mostly GLP-1 based (semaglutide, liraglutide) and the GIP/GLP-1 medicine tirzepatide, which reduce appetite; others include orlistat, naltrexone–bupropion and phentermine.

Qawam never gives doses. It only records what your doctor prescribed and reminds you. Never start, stop or change a dose without your doctor.
🔬Pathway 3

Endoscopic

Procedures done through a scope with no external cuts, sitting between medications and surgery: the intragastric balloon (temporary, ~6 months), endoscopic sleeve gastroplasty (internal stitches reshape the stomach), and aspiration therapy. Recovery is usually quick, followed by a staged diet and lifestyle work for lasting results.

🏥Pathway 4

Surgical — bariatric surgery

The most effective and durable treatment for severe obesity, often improving or resolving type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure and fatty liver. The two most common operations are sleeve gastrectomy and Roux-en-Y gastric bypass. Surgery is a lifelong partnership: pre-op work-up, a carefully staged post-op diet, daily vitamins for life, and regular follow-up.

Questions
Frequently asked
Is Qawam a medical device or a diet plan?
No. Qawam is a non-medical companion for personal tracking and education. It does not diagnose, predict, prescribe calories or drug doses, or replace professional care. Everything it shows is descriptive.
Which pathway is right for me?
It depends on your starting point, health conditions and goals — and the pathways combine rather than compete. This decision should always be shared with a specialist team. Qawam helps you learn about each and prepare good questions.
Does the app track my medication doses?
Yes — you enter the medicine and the exact dose your doctor prescribed, set a reminder, and can export it to your phone calendar. Qawam never suggests or changes a dose.
What happens to my data?
Everything you log stays on your device by default — no account required. You can export a copy anytime or permanently delete everything. You choose when and with whom to share a doctor summary.
Is it free, and does it work offline?
Yes to both. Qawam is completely free and installable — open it in your browser or add it to your home screen, and it keeps working offline. It is fully bilingual with right-to-left support.
